The Myth of the Jack of All Trades

Master of None

Most people only remember the first half of the quote:

"Jack of all trades, master of none."

For years, that line stopped me.

It made me believe that having interests in Web Development, Immunology, Finance, Worldbuilding, and a dozen other fields meant I lacked focus. That switching between building an app one day and writing sci-fi lore the next made me unserious.

But then I learned the full quote:

"A jack of all trades is a master of none, but oftentimes better than a master of one."

And everything changed.

Intersections Are Where Innovation Lives

The world doesn’t reward narrow boxes anymore.

It rewards intersections — the places where fields collide and unexpected ideas emerge.

The evolutionary logic of biology.

The precision of code.

The structure of storytelling.

The leverage loops of finance.

When you blend these, you stop playing inside one system… and start building your own.

That’s when the fear dissolves.

You realize curiosity wasn’t a distraction — it was the compass.

The Journey of a Polymath (Or at Least, Someone Trying)

This blog isn’t a guru’s podium.

It’s a documentation of becoming — my attempt to step into polymathy one system, one skill, one curiosity at a time.

I’m not trying to master isolated fields.
I’m trying to understand the threads that tie them together — how technology shapes behavior, how biology shapes systems, how stories shape decisions, how money shapes possibility.

These aren’t separate interests.
They’re one conversation.

They’re modules — combinable, remixable, evolutionary.

And as I evolve, this blog will evolve with me.

The only constant here is curiosity.

A Question for You

If you’ve ever felt “too scattered,” “too broad,” or “too curious,” you’re not broken — you’re a generalist in a world built by specialists.

But the future will belong to people who stand at intersections.

So let me ask you:

What are you a master of none in?
