Pulse May 2025

Devlog 0 — What is Pulse?

The idea, the scrapped start, and why I began again.

The plan was simple: build a site that tracks the websites I visit frequently so I know when they're down. The idea came from constantly checking my college results portal and government websites, never knowing if the site was down or just slow. Frustrating enough to build something about it.

Like most projects I start these days, I kicked things off with vibe coding — minimal prompts, just vibing to get a feel for what I was building. Used Stitch by Google for the frontend design and Cursor for the code. Honestly? It looked decent. Clean UI, good vibes.

But here's the thing — the frontend looked great and the backend didn't exist yet. I couldn't explain a single decision in the codebase. So I scrapped it and started over with my own hands, using Claude as a mentor to guide me rather than generate code for me.

What I learned: vibe coding takes the fun out of programming. You're not really coding, you're just waiting. It's less vibe coding, more vibe waiting.
